Instant replies
kill the thought.

When a response arrives in two seconds, it disappears in three. You scroll past it. You fire something back without sitting with what was just said to you.

A message that arrives eight hours later is different. You read it in the morning, before your first meeting. Or at 11pm when the day is quiet. It lands in a different context than the one you were in when you sent it. It has weight.

We built intentional delay into the core of how TextBack works — not as a limitation, but because the timing is part of what makes a conversation real.
